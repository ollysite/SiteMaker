About ASPBM

Uniting Asia-Pacific, driving patient blood management for enhanced outcomes.

Our Vision

To bring the life-changing benefits of Patient Blood Management to every person across the Asia-Pacific.

Our Mission

Driving advancements in patient blood management (PBM) through education and collaborative initiatives, we foster partnerships among healthcare professionals, patients, organizations and advocates across the Asia-Pacific region to enhance healthcare quality and efficiency.

Our Story

The Asia-Pacific region is a vibrant and diverse landscape, home to the world's two largest populations. Despite the immense variations in culture, economy, and healthcare systems, a common need persists: ensuring access to fundamental human rights, good medical care, and a robust economy. Patient Blood Management (PBM) is a crucial part of this equation.

Recognizing the wide disparities in PBM implementation across our region—from well-established national programs to a complete lack of awareness—the Asia-Pacific Society for Patient Blood Management (ASPBM) was founded in 2015. Our motivation was clear: to unite people with a common interest in PBM and to bring its life-changing benefits to everyone.

What is Patient Blood Management?

Patient Blood Management (PBM) is a patient-centered, systematic, evidence-based approach to improve patient outcomes by managing and preserving a patient's own blood, while promoting patient safety and empowerment.

- Shander et. al. A Global Definition of Patient Blood Management, A&A, Feb 2022.

PBM is a goldmine for health economics. It provides superior medical care with the patient as the focus, while simultaneously reducing costs. By optimizing a patient's own blood, we can improve outcomes, enhance patient autonomy, and boost the economic efficiency of healthcare systems.

Our Endeavours

ASPBM is dedicated to bridging the gaps in PBM awareness and implementation throughout the Asia-Pacific. Our work began with a strong focus on education, sharing vital knowledge and expertise through scientific meetings and conferences. As our organization has grown, so have our aspirations.

We are now expanding our focus to include research and other collaborations to drive new discoveries and foster a stronger, more connected community. Through these endeavours, we aim to enhance PBM implementation where it has already taken root, and to bring it to places where it has not been, so that all people and their economies can benefit from this critical approach to modern medicine.

We look forward to working together in this shared mission.
